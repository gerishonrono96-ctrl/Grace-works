Who We Are

About Grace Center PEFA Church

Grace Center PEFA Church is a Pentecostal Evangelistic Fellowship of Africa (PEFA) congregation located in Ex-Margaret, Rongai, Nakuru County, Kenya. We are a community of believers committed to worship, discipleship, and reaching our neighborhood with the love of Christ.

From Bible study to fellowship gatherings through the week, Grace Center exists to help every person grow in their walk with God — whether you're taking your first step of faith or have walked with the Lord for years.

Our Vision

We PEFA Grace Center exist: To see people saved, transformed holistically by The Word of God.

Our Mission

Making believers of all the nations and equipping them to become disciples of Christ and become fruitful in the Kingdom.

From the Pulpit

Messages & Teaching

A few of the messages recently shared at Grace Center. Tap a title to read.

"And do not bring sorrow to God's Holy Spirit by the way you live. Remember, he has identified you as his own, guaranteeing that you will be saved on the day of redemption." — Ephesians 4:30 (NLT)

Eight things that quietly weaken our walk with the Spirit and dull our spiritual sensitivity:

  • Offence — harboring hurt closes off the flow of the Spirit.
  • Disobedience — resisting what God has clearly said.
  • Spiritual negligence — neglecting prayer, worship, and God's Word dulls spiritual sensitivity.
  • Pride — shuts out the work of the Holy Spirit. Judgment doesn't come instantly, but pride is always a warning sign.
  • Desire for people's approval — you cannot be Spirit-led and crave attention at the same time. Anointing rests on those who love the secret place; those who crave public prayer are often not praying in private.
  • Fear — fear kills obedience.
  • Lust — destroys purity; Samson and Ahab show how desire can destroy purpose and legacy.
  • Excuses — like Saul, we can justify disobedience rather than repent of it.
"God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble." — James 4:6 (NLT)
"Fearing people is a dangerous trap, but trusting the Lord means safety." — Proverbs 29:25 (NLT)
"You may believe there's nothing wrong with what you are doing, but keep it between yourself and God." — Romans 14:22 (NLT)

Saul's Excuse

When confronted, Saul answered: he saw his men scattering, Samuel had not arrived as promised, and the Philistines were massing for battle — so he felt compelled to offer the sacrifice himself before Samuel came (1 Samuel 13:11–12). Fear and impatience produced disobedience dressed up as necessity.

"Seek the Kingdom of God above all else, and live righteously, and he will give you everything you need." — Matthew 6:33 (NLT)

A Kingdom seeker is someone whose priority is seeking godliness, and who engages actively in the spiritual life rather than seeking the approval of people.

Qualities of a Kingdom Seeker

  • Right priority — seeks God's Kingdom first, and with purpose.
  • Doesn't look for man's approval; when God's Kingdom becomes your priority, you stop struggling — He takes care of your needs.
  • Hungers for God's presence (Psalm 42:1).
  • Carries a distinct anointing — like David and Joseph, who carried God's favor from the pit and the prison all the way to the palace.
  • Anointing doesn't mean an absence of challenges — it means God's presence carries us through them (Psalm 23:4).
  • When we seek Him, He releases grace and power to us.
  • Kingdom seekers influence their generation (Acts 13:36) — we are blessed so that we can serve others.

Kingdom seekers win souls, advance, and serve others — and they receive Kingdom rewards. When we seek God in our generation, He blesses us for a lifetime.

Divine Favour Brings

  • Blessings
  • Wisdom
  • Peace
  • Spiritual authority
  • Eternal reward

The Response

Make a decision to seek God. Seek His righteousness. Desire His presence. Serve with purpose. Serve His Kingdom.

What Is This Light That Shines?

It is the Word of God that brings transformation — the light that attracts people to God. It is not wealth, fame, or position; it is faithfully serving God and influencing others positively according to His purpose. Every believer is called to greatness, because we are created in the image of God and empowered by the Holy Spirit.

Greatness Comes by Knowing Your Identity

  • Greatness is not about becoming famous — it's about becoming everything God created you to be.
  • You are a child of God, created with purpose.
  • Great people know who they are in Christ Jesus.
  • Greatness requires a relationship with God.

How Do We Build a Relationship With God?

  • Prayer, worship, and studying the Word build spiritual strength.
  • Those who walk closely with God receive wisdom and direction.

Greatness Demands Character

  • Integrity
  • Humility
  • Faithfulness

Greatness is found in serving others (Mark 10:43–48). Whoever wants to be great must be a servant — in the family, the church, and the community. Service opens doors to influence and favor.

Greatness Needs Faith and Courage

Great people face obstacles, but they refuse to give up. They trust God even when circumstances seem impossible.

Conclusion

  • Know your identity in Christ
  • Walk closely with God
  • Build godly character
  • Serve others
  • Live by faith
  • Leave a legacy

Every believer is called to greatness — because we are made in God's image, for His purpose, empowered by His Spirit.

"And I will ask the Father, and he will give you another Advocate, who will never leave you. He is the Holy Spirit, who leads into all truth. The world cannot receive him, because it isn't looking for him and doesn't recognize him. But you know him, because he lives with you now and later will be in you. No, I will not abandon you as orphans — I will come to you." — John 14:16–18 (NLT)

He's a Person

  • Not a force, energy, or feeling.
  • A personal helper given by God.
  • He's God dwelling in believers.

What Shows He's a Person

  • He was present at creation.
  • He was present in the Old Testament, manifesting through specific people; in the New Testament, He lives in every believer to empower them.
  • He speaks.
  • He is a Redeemer — the one who led Israel through the sea and made His name known forever.
  • He can be grieved.
  • He has intelligence.
"Then they remembered those days of old when Moses led his people out of Egypt. They cried out, 'Where is the one who brought Israel through the sea, with Moses as their shepherd? Where is the one who sent his Holy Spirit to be among his people?'" — Isaiah 63:11 (NLT)
"And do not bring sorrow to God's Holy Spirit by the way you live. Remember, he has identified you as his own, guaranteeing that you will be saved on the day of redemption." — Ephesians 4:30 (NLT)

Works of the Holy Spirit

  • Empowers for evangelism.
  • Teaches the truth.
  • Glorifies Jesus.
  • Instructs and speaks (Acts 8:29).
  • Gives us power to overcome temptation (Galatians 5).
  • Causes us to love God (Romans 5:5).
  • Discloses Jesus to us (John 16:13–14).

Signs That You Have the Holy Spirit

  • You have peace — you can be in a storm, but the difference is the presence of God. It is being calm in times of trouble because of the assurance of the Holy Spirit (Colossians 3:16).
  • Sensitive to sin (John 16:8).
  • Discernment — a greater sensitivity to God's leading (Isaiah 30:21; Revelation 3:7).
  • Clarity of the Word.
  • You receive God's wisdom — God gives wisdom beyond human understanding (James 1:5).
  • The fruit of the Holy Spirit (Galatians 5:22–23).

Egypt = Bondage, Sin, Old Life, Fleshly Comfort

We are not to go back to Egypt (Exodus 16:3). We need to be fully transformed in the house of God — the dangerous place is being in God's house with an unrenewed heart. We need to remove any hidden sin.

Israel were delivered physically, but their hearts were not — they had left the bondage, but the bondage had not left them. If we don't work out our salvation, we gain nothing; if we don't change wholly, delay becomes inevitable. There is nothing good left in Egypt.

Wilderness Is Not Punishment — It Is Preparation

The wilderness exposes what is in the heart (Proverbs 12:3). Wilderness means process, testing, and transformation (Deuteronomy 8:2).

  • It tests the heart.
  • It breaks the slavery mentality.
  • It builds dependence on God — manna came daily to teach trust (Exodus 16:14).
  • It removes pride.

Pain is not the point of the process — the wilderness is a place of transformation, and it has an end. It is where God builds our dependence on Him.

Don't Die Between Egypt and the Promised Land

Three things kept many from entering Canaan:

  • Unbelief
  • Fear
  • Complaining

Canaan represents maturity, process, and victory. God uses the wilderness season to transform slaves into sons (James 1:2–3). Hardship makes people remember Egypt, and neglecting God's Word is what draws believers back toward Egypt in their hearts.

What Jesus Used to Overcome Temptation

Jesus overcame even in physical need. When we neglect the Word, we grow weak spiritually.

Bottom Line

There is no easy, effortless life in Christianity — growth is found in the process, not around it.
